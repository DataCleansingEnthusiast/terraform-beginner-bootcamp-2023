Welcome to Alaska

Alaska, the largest state in the United States, is a land of stunning natural beauty and rugged wilderness. It is known for its breathtaking landscapes, including vast glaciers, towering mountains, and pristine forests.

Portage Glacier

Portage Glacier is one of Alaska's most famous glaciers, located in the Chugach National Forest. This massive glacier, with its striking blue ice, is a popular destination for tourists and nature enthusiasts. Visitors can take a boat tour on Portage Lake to get up close to the glacier and witness the spectacular calving events when chunks of ice break off into the water.

Denali National Park

Denali National Park and Preserve is a true wilderness gem in Alaska. It's home to Denali, North America's highest peak, formerly known as Mount McKinley.

The park covers six million acres of unspoiled wilderness and is a haven for wildlife, including grizzly bears, wolves, and moose. Visitors can explore the park's rugged terrain, go hiking, or take a scenic flightseeing tour to witness the grandeur of Denali and its surrounding peaks.

Alaska, with its unique landscapes and outdoor adventures, offers a once-in-a-lifetime experience for those who seek the beauty of the wild and the tranquility of nature.
